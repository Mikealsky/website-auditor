# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Non-technical small business owners (cafes, salons, tradespeople, local services). They don't know what CLS or LCP means. They're checking their own website from a desktop browser, probably once, looking for a quick verdict and a clear to-do list. Their context: mild anxiety about their web presence, time-poor, suspicious of jargon.

## Product Purpose

Website Auditor scans a business's website and returns performance, SEO, and design scores alongside a plain-English prioritised fix list and downloadable PDF. Success looks like a business owner who understands exactly what to do next, without needing to hire an agency to interpret the results.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, fast, direct. No hand-holding, no jargon, no filler. Gets to the result and tells you what to fix. Confident and clear the way a good mechanic is — not flashy, just right.

## Anti-references

- Cold SaaS / enterprise dashboard: dark navy, dense data tables, Notion-style monochrome, feels like internal tooling
- Generic AI tool aesthetic: cream/sand/beige backgrounds, glassmorphism, neutral-everything, no personality — the saturated 2026 AI default
- Overly playful / startup-cute: pastel gradients, bouncy illustrations, feels like a productivity app for teenagers

## Design Principles

1. **Verdict first** — the user should know how their site is doing in under 3 seconds of reading the results page
2. **Plain English always** — every metric, every label, every CTA should be readable by someone with no technical background
3. **Earned confidence** — the UI should feel like it knows what it's talking about without showing off; authority through clarity, not decoration
4. **Speed signals trust** — loading state, transitions, and layout shifts directly affect perceived credibility; a janky tool auditing jank is self-defeating
5. **One action at a time** — each screen has a single primary job; don't compete for attention across columns

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the floor. Body text must hit 4.5:1 against its background. Non-technical users include older adults who may have reduced contrast sensitivity. Avoid color-only status encoding (always pair with icon or label).
